<p><strong><em><span style="font-size:115%"> Welcome to Lyndvhar! </span></em></strong></p>
<p> This lore primer serves as an introduction to Lyndvhar's setting.
<br><br>
As always, note that this lore primer shouldn't be treated as gospel. Don't stress too much about trying to "get it all right" - it's far more fun to spark conflict through contradiction, and to explore the many unique interpretations that characters can have.
<br><br>
Only the broadest strokes have been applied onto this canvas. There's plenty of supplemental lore hidden throughout Lyndvhar's in-game world: level a keen eye towards the dungeons, tomes, events, and descriptions of examined items, if you wish to uncover Beowricke's secrets. 
<br><br>
If you have questions relating to in-game mechanics, you can refer to here - https://azurepeak.miraheze.org/ - for more information regarding the mechanics of the upstream, and more.</p>

Once a dispute over the succession of the Ruby Throne has spiraled into a catastrophe the likes of which the realm had never seen before, resulting in its fracture and separation into warring states of warlords.

<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> MYTHOS OF BEOWRICKE </span></strong></summary>

	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> OVERVIEW OF THE DIVINE </span></strong>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE FATHER OF THE ORTHODOX </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#007fff; font-size:115%"> "..." </span>
		<br>
		Psydon is worshipped as the creator of ontological reality, and the only true force of goodness that Beowricke ever knew. It was He who gifted man-and-mer with not only existence, but the capacity to emote like Him. When the Archdaemons tore open reality and threatened to destroy Beowricke in the Bloodwake, it was Him who undertook the ultimate sacrifice to save the world He had come to love. 
<br><br>
Nowadays, most believe that Psydon is gone: yet, an ardent minority continues to remain loyal in the hopes that He may yet return to save His world once more. His children might be bereft of miracles and answers, but they continue to endure - even at the end of the world.
<br><br>
		Psydonic worshippers are in the minority, and are generally ostracized by most kingdoms. The Orthodoxy teaches the most widespread form of Psydonic worship, though there are countless other forms practiced throughout the world. 
<br><br>
Lyndvhar currently houses a cohort of the Holy Psydonic Inquisition, as part of an old diplomatic affair with Valoria before the Ruby War.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE PANTHEON </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#ffd700; font-size:115%"> "WITH ORDER, WE REIGN; AND WITH FAITH, WE PROSPER." </span>
		<br>
		The Pantheon are the dominant faith of Beowricke: ten deities who control many aspects of reality. After the Bloodwake, most of the Pantheon went into a deep slumber to recover from the damages sustained during such a calamitous event. 
<br><br>
		<br><br>
		Pantheonic worshippers are in the majority, and have remained as such for over a millenium's time. Almost every kingdom in Beowricke houses a church that preaches the Pantheon's teachings, taken directly from the Mother Church's tomes.
<br><br>
Lyndvhar is no different, being in the heart of the Pantheonic world. While Astrata is traditionally worshipped as the Pantheon's supreme deity, the Mother Church oft-emphasizes that all ten deities should be given equal reverance. Hence, it isn't uncommon to see an Astratan praying to Ravox for courage in the midst of battle, nor to witness a Malumite cursing out Dendor for overmuddying a carriage's intended pathway. 
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE ARCHDAEMONS </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#800080; font-size:115%"> "WHAT IS A GOD, IF NOT A MAN WHO HAS RECOGNIZED THE FOLLY OF MORTALITY?" </span>
		<br>
		The Archdaemons are the most sinful aspects of mortality, and - as alleged by its worshippers - the true divine beings of Beowricke. Unlike the Pantheon, these four deities were never divine to begin with: instead, they were created from the combined sins of mortal hands across the realm. They attempted to take control of reality in an event known as the Bloodwake, where they were defeated and casted back into the Hells.
<br><br>
Zizo is the patron of ambition, Baotha offers debauchery, Graggar dominates the weak, and Matthios tricks those into immense greed - yet, their worshippers seldom know the price that must be paid to these daemonic entities. While the Mother Church has desperately tried to erase them from public knowledge, there have always been underground cults to their hands since the Bloodwake.
		<br><br>
		Cultists vary from region-to-region, and from person-to-person. Public support of a daemon is heretical, often hunted down by those who would never see them rise again. As such, many cultists are underground, preaching in darkness while publicly pretending to praise the Pantheon.
<br><br>
		<br>
	</details>

	<br><br>
</details>


<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> A ROUGH TIMELINE OF BEOWRICKE </span></strong></summary>

	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> NOTEWORTHY EVENTS </span></strong>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> CREATION - ? BB  </strong></summary>
		<br>
		Astrata, feeling lonely in the nonexistent void, crafts nine siblings from her own body, surrendering aspects of creation she once held in order to create them. With this newfound pantheon of ten, they forge with Malum's hammer the realm of Beowricke, and all lives within it.
		<br><br>
		The divine would have much more open involvement in the affairs of mortals, often giving great boons to their followers, with some even theorizing the creation of demigods with some mortals.
		<br><br>
		This peace would not last, as it was tumultuous at best with their creations: many acted against the wishes of the Divine, and committed horrific sins in the name of these deities, all boiling into the creations of much darker forces.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE BLOODWAKE - YEAR 0 </strong></summary>
		<br>
		A new threat had begun bubbling from the thoughts and minds of those living in the realm. The ARCHDAEMONS, those personifying the very worst aspects of mortalkind, began to emerge. With every single sinful act committed, the sin that was done would strengthen their existence. 
		<br><br>
		With their growth in power, they waged war against the very PANTHEON. The resulting clash of Gods and Daemon nearly tore the realm of Beowricke asunder, bringing it into an age of war- of destruction. These times are known in legend as the BLOODWAKE. 
		<br><br>
		During this time the AASIMAR were constructed to fight the legions of lesser daemons that the Four casted from their own power. Cultists would begin to emerge, sacrificing their very souls or bodies to give to the daemons. Through these unholy acts, the TIEFLING would emerge as enslaved thralls of ZIZO. To host his own legion, the Archdaemon GRAGGAR would corrupt elvish cultists, transforming them into ORCS and GOBLINS as we know them today. During this war in a climactic battle, the ARCHDAEMONS would be defeated and casted back into the pits of the hells whence they came, where their authority and power was effectively reduced to nothing. They would be trapped for eternity in their hole, rotting and forgotten.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE GILDED AGE - 0 AB to 1500 AB </strong></summary>
		<br>
		With the world in ruin after the Bloodwake, the Pantheon would retreat back to their realms, horribly weakened and at worst left nigh powerless. Even to this dae, it is believed that the Pantheon has never truly recovered and never will. Both sides left behind the Aasimar and the Tiefling as the descendants and proof of the Bloodwake. This act of retreat left Beowricke effectively unattended and at the mercy of its inheritors- the mortals.
		<br><br>
		Many would set up their own fiefs, and begin the slow reconstruction and recovery of the land. As time progressed, legends were crafted, stories told, and lands would rise and fall. Very little is written of these times, as it took hundreds of years for a true recovery to be completed.
		<br><br>
		In this Era, the most notable realms would be forged within the timespan of around 1000-1500 AB, the final one being the Lyndhardtian Empire. With its creation and declaration of a new era, many consider the end of the Gilded Age to be 1500 AB.
		<br><br>
		Most of the occult went underground, being forced into hiding after such a disastrous defeat, and with no assistance from their daemon masters.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE RUBY AGE - 1500 AB to 1694 AB </strong></summary>
		<br>
		With the passing of the year 1500 AB, a man rose to prominence in a rather unknown city state along the Splitstern Sea, and this man was named Naexidor. A rather powerful arcyne conduit, skilled swordsman, and masterful tactician, his elvish blood yearned to prove his dominance, and with the onset of raids from nearby orcish tribes- his plans for conquest were set in motion.
		<br><br>
		His strength and cunning prowess on the battlefield led to successful destruction of the orc tribes, casting them into almost complete obscurity, relegated to minor tribes in the far western plains and mountains.  But the conquest did not stop there, as Naexidor set his sights upon the north, the east, and parts of the west, slowly but surely conquering city after city, and nation after nation.
		<br><br>
		This nigh almost unstoppable tidal wave of conquest under the Ruby Banner of Naexidor led to the forging of one of the greatest empires that Beowricke had ever seen, the Lyndhardtian Empire. Naexidor's descendants would go on to hold this empire together quite firmly, expanding upon it with each passing coronation. It seemed like there would be no end to their strength or power. However, in 1694 AB, that would all change.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE RUBY WAR - 1694 AB to 1704 AB </strong></summary>
		<br>
		The Lyndhardtian Empire ruled the continent of Lewyn for nearly two-hundred long years by the beginning of the year 1694AB, but all empires eventually meet their end. During a military parade in Aevislan, the current Empress- Camille Lyndhardtia- fell comatose for an unknown reason. She never woke up. With this death, no true direct heir of the realm could be seen. And with tensions already beginning to boil between upper aristocracy of Lyndhardtia, thus began the sudden and abrupt collapse of authority.
		<br><br>
		The resulting civil war, known by many as the Ruby War, would tear the entire empire apart. Entire villages were wiped off the map, cities were razed, and borders were constantly fluctuating. Neighboring realms took full advantage of the chaos, swooping in for their own slices of the rotting cake. Over the course of ten years, four true claimants would emerge and still to this very dae fight for their right to the rotting Ruby Throne- or what's left of it.
		<br><br>
		Baleron, the illegitimate bastard successor of the Empress herself, was in the north in a campaign against the Rhaenvali Confederation at the beginning of the Ruby War, and consolidated the northern territories for himself- declaring himself the true Emperor of Lyndhardtia. Laurence, a powerful Archmage with heavy ties to the Eyrie College, declared a successor state in the south- a full-fledged Magocracy- and began a terror campaign against all who opposed him, razing entire cities to the ground. Gunthard, the well known Duke of the Draguippe, quickly amassed his own powerbase and took control of the centralized heartlands of the Empire, declaring his own successor state out of the remains of the capital. Vladislav, a general having been fighting a holy war against the orcish hordes of the southwest, was anointed as the true holy mortal aspect of the Luminary by a regional papal power, and carved out his own small territory of the collapsing realm to rule as its Luminous Emperor.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> THE CURRENT - 1704 AB </strong></summary>
		<br>
		In the current year, things have darkened for the worse. Most methods of communication have been lost amongst remaining cities and towns, leading to complete isolation for most settlements across the ruins of Lyndhardtia. A horde of orcs shred through the southern lands of Lewyn, their brutalization by the Lyndhardtian armies no longer a threat, while Rhaenish raiders from the north pillage and destroy what riches they can take from their old adversary.
		<br><br>
		And yet, despite all of this, some lands still persist in the darkness of the aftermath, trying to weave what little lyfe they can in the remains of what was once opulent and pristine. 
		<br><br>
		Due to the mass death, famine, and destruction seen, forces and signs not seen since the Bloodwake have begun to form. A rise in undead sightings have begun to emerge, with some graveyards nearly completely emptying out with the living dead. A golden age for piracy and banditry has begun, stemmed by the lack of any central authority. An increase in addiction, and other sins against the flesh. It has become increasingly clear to many superstitious commonfolk that this collapse is bringing about something far, far worse.
		<br><br>
		What about you, lowly fool? Where do you stand in this midst, actor?

<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> POWERS OF BEOWRICKE </span></strong></summary>

	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> NOTEWORTHY REALMS </span></strong>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> LYNDHARDTIA </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Ruby Empire, Naexidor's Realm</em>
		<br><br>
		Once the most thriving Empire in all of Beowricke, spanning an entire continent’s worth of strength. Having assimilated most neighbors under Naexidor’s Conquest, it began a slow decline that lasted nearly two-hundred years until the Ruby War culminated in its shattering. Many have been profiteering off of its destruction. 
<br><br>
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> GRENZELHOFT </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Imperiate</em>
		<br><br>
		An empire that exists across the sea in Yoheon. Their cities are built from marble and stone, and operate in a very archaic and strange way of noble elective to decide who controls the Imperiate.
<br><br>
Grenzelhoft is actually a massive mess of multiple counties, duchies, and lesser Kingdoms all together in one monstrous confederation. It’s much more well known for being the only realm in Beowricke to refuse allegiance to any form of divine patron.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> VALORIA </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Republica Valoria, Birthplace of Psydonia</em>
		<br><br>
		The Valorians are a hardy militaristic people, who had lived in their part of Lewyn for centuries. With the onset of Naexidor, they forged a pact and alliance with him out of desperation against the threat of his storm of swords.
<br><br>
It is the only kingdom of notable size that worships Psydon, and - as a theocratically governed merchant republic - it is ruled by the Archbishop of Psydon and an elite caste of merchants. With the recent destruction of the Empire as an entity they’ve begun to succumb to repeated invasions from the orcish hordes of the north.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> KHAZUMIA </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Khaliphate</em>
		<br><br>
		Khazumia is a nation in Yoheon dominated by shifting desert sands, northern mountains that border Grenzelhoft, and lush savannas and oasis that cling to the coast. Governed by the magocratic elite known as the Vaktasi, they retain a relatively neutral stance to other realms in Yoheon. With an emphasis on meritocratic strength, Khazumia is most renowned for some of the best swordsmen in Beowricke, and many powerful mages. There are rumours abound about the Vaktasi, and some claim that the most powerful of their cabal can see the future, though this is still debated by outsiders.
<br><br> 
Decades of invasion from Forvheipal, a destabilization of northern land by a skirmish with Grenzelhoft, and the exodus of its talent in search for more opportunities outside of the Khaliphate has put Khazumia in a gradual state of decline.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> GREENCREST </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Crestborne</em>
		<br><br>
		An Elven-controlled kingdom to the far southwest of Lyndhardtia, in Lewyn. Controlled by a vastly wealthy Elvish aristocracy, all other races are effectively barred from upper castes of its heavily feudalistic society.
<br><br>
In recent passings due to the collapse of Lyndhardtia as an entity, it has struggled just as much as Valoria- a sharp decline in its societal influence and a slow rampant destruction by orcish hordes with the power of the Ruby Throne lost.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> FORVHEIPAL </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Slavers of Yoheon's Southeast</em>
		<br><br>
		A rather brutal nation to the east of Khazumia in Yoheon. Infamous for its extensive use of the slave trade and brutal authoritarian caste system, it boasts some of the most ruthless mercenaries, traders, and warriors across Beowricke.
<br><br>
Considered outwardly aggressive by those not in its borders to all its neighbors, it still has many guilds that do business with outlying territories, the most famous one being the Zaprixys Order, which sells its slave-warriors to illicit black markets in Lewyn abroad. It is considered a staunch rival to Yanshen.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> RHAENVAL </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Grey Horde</em>
		<br><br>
		Though the name "The Grey Horde" is what many call it, it would be derogative to what it actually is. Rhaenval is a nomadic confederation of multiple tribes originating from the northwest of Lewyn.
<br><br>
 Having received the bulk of Lyndhardtian invasions at their zenith, began a direct invasion of reclamation against Baleron for the lost territories of former tribes of the region. It is not uncommon to see Rhaenish pirates and raiders sacking coastal settlements across previously controlled Lyndhardtian land to pillage the remains.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> ZYBANTU </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Raj of the North</em>
		<br><br>
		Zybantu, resides across the ocean in Yoheon as Grenzelhoft's greatest rival entity. A people of proud heritage of the northern deserts and lush jungles, they accumulate wealth via their trade of illicit materials and mercenary companies. It is often involved in direct trade competition and even border skirmishes with the Grenzelhoftians, owing to their long standing rivalry.
<br><br>
Zybantu in the current year is one of the strongest realms in Beowricke, with a stranglehold over the exportation of exotic spices, and silks. In more recent months their corsairs have been seen in more prominence across the Splitstern Sea in Lewyn, bringing many to fear they may attempt an across-the-sea incursion into Lewyn through Lyndhardtian ruins.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> YANSHEN </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Saltlanders</em>
		<br><br>
		The Yanshen peoples are the people who live in a relatively consistent militant state out of necessity in eastern Yoheon. Controlled by an Emperor, declared the Celestial of Heaven, it operates off of the principled tenets and acts written in the Divine Spirit, a form of guiding material for them to follow. The Yansheneze are one of the more staunch opposition forces against Forvheipal, consistently finding themself in skirmish and war against them.
<br><br>
The Yansheneze will tell outsiders of a beautiful landscape, peppered with bountiful land and the longest and largest rivers in all of Beowricke. Such peace cannot be savored it seems, however, as recently in act of defiance against a recent coronation of a Celestial of the Xinyi heritage the Xuefeng Family attempted a coup of the reigning nobility, albeit failing miserably and being forced to flee in exile in different directions, some notably finding refuge in coastal cities of Lewyn.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> ACKRINVAL </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Ruins of the Dead</em>
		<br><br>
		A region of northeastern Yoheon mired in mystique and danger. It is said that a dark cloud hangs across this region of the world, and the dead do not stay dead.
<br><br>
Considered cursed by many, nobody ventures here- and those who do are never seen again. Many theorize this may have been where the final battles of the Bloodwake occurred, and that there is a direct pit to the Hells in the centre of its accursed lands.
		<br>
	</details>

	<br>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> MISCELLANIOUS FIEFDOMS </span></strong>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> AEVISLAN </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Capital of Lyndhardtia</em>
		<br><br>
		Aevislan is the former capital of the Lyndhardtian Empire, considered by many to be the greatest empire in all of Beowricke's protracted history. Its many riches, vast winding roads, and massive cathedral-like home to the reigning family of the empire were considered some of the largest and most opulent in all of the realms.
<br><br>
However, not everything lasts forever. Now a shell of its former self, it is ruled over by Gunthard in nigh tyranny. Despite his popularity beforehand with the common folks, with his declaration for the throne after the onset of the Ruby War, many of its roads lie dormant and quiet, the trade having ceased. Most riches and men went to the war effort, leaving little left. Anything else is mere secondhand testiment.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> LYNDVHAR </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The City of Meritocracy</em>
		<br><br>
		Nestled in the Draguippe along the coast of the Splitstern Sea, the city of Lyndvhar has almost definitely seen better days. Once a hotspot of imperial activity and one of the most rich trading hubs of the eastern coastline, its connections to the outside beyond the borders of the Canover Range (the mountains around the city) and past Mount Decapitation have been all but severed. In an emergency act the current ruling aristocracy of the port have taken direct control of political affairs in joint cooperation with the Luminary Church of Lyndvhar, effectively declaring its independence as a city-state until the current situation of Lyndhardtia becomes more clear. 
<br><br>
This has not spared them the dangers rising amongst their territories, however. With the loss of outside support anarchy and wilderness have been creeping in- a few of these being the Lilac Bay being lost to a raiding band of Rhaenish corsairs, the Terrorbog fort being lost to the murk, and the fort residing in the Draguippe being overrun with an orcish party. Nonetheless the city stands strong, cowering behind the walls and surviving with its trade to the other continent.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> NAEXIDORIA </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Conqueror-Named, City of Fame</em>
		<br><br>
		Naexidoria, as the name implies, was the northern city of which Naexidor based his northern conquests: hence, the name. It has seen better daes with the civil war, however. 
<br><br>
Outside of its famous namesake, its connection to Lyndvhar was lost when the northern roads were overgrown from poor management, and its line of communication gone dark. Little is known about its current situation.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> CALMIRIXIA </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Homeland of the Eskallian</em>
		<br><br>
		The swamps and marshes that the lizard Eskallian call home. Once a thriving kingdom before the conquest, now relegated to a conquered province in the southwest.
<br><br>
After it was annexed and organized into a province, there was a large schism between both the Dracanae and the Caiman that has since never fully been solved, causing immense distrust between the two cultural parties.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> KHUUFVALE </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Lost Remains of a Dwarven Hold</em>
		<br><br>
		Khuufvale once stood as a metallurgic community, neighboring the city of Lyndvhar in the pits of Decapitation's volcanic activity. It nearly three decades ago went completely quiet, however- and those who approach are never seen again.
<br><br>
Brave adventurers try their hand at entry into the connected pieces of its hold, and what little information has been gathered is hordes of minotaurs, collections of undead, and even worse.
		<br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> WEOCILYN </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Barony of Crimson</em>
		<br><br>
		A mildly renowned barony to the west of Lyndvhar, sharing rather friendly ties with the ruling nobility of the city. Many a time the families of both cities have visited one another for banquet and marriage.
<br><br>
Disaster has recently stabbed its underbelly, however, with the civil war. In an act that many saw as a disgrace, Baleron sieged the barony of Weocilyn and torched most of it to the ground- leaving it a ruined husk. Whether the ruling family survived or not is unknown, but it has put Baleron in direct distaste of the entirety of Lyndvhar's mouths.
		<br>
	</details>

<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> GLOSSARY OF BEOWRICKE </span></strong></summary>

	<details>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> NOTEWORTHY PEOPLES </span></strong>
	<br><br>
		<p><strong> Naexidor </strong> - The founder of the Lyndhardtian Empire. An elven warrior that wielded a powerful hammer imbued with powerful arcyne strength. They themselves were a masterful arcyne conduit, and expert strategist, conquering nearly half the continent of Lewyn. </p>
		<p><strong> Camille </strong> - A half-elf, and the last Empress of the Lyndhardtian Empire, before her untimely and unexplained death. The cause is still unknown, but her death spiraled the realm into chaos. </p>
		<p><strong> Baleron </strong> -  The illegitimate bastard successor of the Empress herself, was in the north in a campaign against the Rhaenvali Confederation at the beginning of the Ruby War, and consolidated the northern territories for himself- declaring himself the true Emperor of Lyndhardtia. </p>
		<p><strong> Laurence </strong> - A powerful Archmage with heavy ties to the Eyrie College, declared a successor state in the south- a full-fledged Magocracy- and began a terror campaign against all who opposed him, razing entire cities to the ground. </p>
